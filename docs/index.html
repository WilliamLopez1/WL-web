PASSION FOR DATA ANALYSIS


Data analysis is currently one of the most important processes in data science since with it it´s possible to collect, process, analyze, visualize and communicate to generate added value with data.

About me

¡Hello world, mi name is William!

I´m an Industrial Engineer trained at the Pedagogical and Technological University of Colombia with international certification as Six Sigma Black Belt.

During the last two years I have been part of analysis teams of productive activities in the agri-food industry.

The passion for data science has made me enter the world of data analysis, developing projects of analysis of the productivity and quality of processes, optimization and EDA, to give value added to organizations.

Today I want to face increasingly complex projects that contribute to my professional growth.

My analysis tools

01.

Excel

Currently the implementation of Power Query, Power Pivot and pivot tables continue to contribute extensively in the treatment of data and in the generation of reports for the communication managerial.

02.

Python

The best language for data manipulation with libraries like pandas and numpy, and the development of artificial intelligence models. Every day I learn something new from this comprehensive and widely applicable tool.

03.

R & R Studio

My preferred language for visualization and statistical analysis, for it´s simplicity that offer libraries like dplyr and ggplot 2 that allow extraction, cleaning and transformation as well as storytelling with R markdown and Shiny.

04.

Power BI

A very practical and effective visualization and communication tool due to it´s intuitive handling that together with the DAX application provide ease in the generation of reports optimizing the development of Business Intelligence.

05.

SQL Server

Its use is essential in the manipulation, standardization and recovery of data, in addition, the practicality for the generation of dataframes and relational databases allows robust database manipulation.

06.

Statistics

Mastery of statistical concepts such as inference, estimation, correlation, and hypothesis testing is key to performing EDA and the correct interpretation of data that allows the formulation of models that adjust to reality.
